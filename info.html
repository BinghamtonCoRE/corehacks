About

CoREhacks' Premiere Hackathon

February 5-7 ignites a weekend devoted to 29 members sinking 36 hours into learning, building, and creating unique CoRE projects. Mentors and industry representatives from CoRE will also join the fray to lend expertise and share wisdom. The event will take place in the CoRE Lounge for a centralized, communal hacking experience. Expect food, swag, and timeless memories.

FAQ

What is a hackathon?
Great question! A hackathon is best described as an "invention marathon". Anyone who has an interest in technology [or completing their CoRE projects in CoREhacks' case] attends a hackathon to learn, build and share their creations over the course of a weekend in a relaxed and welcoming atmosphere. You don’t have to be a programmer and you certainly don’t have to be majoring in CS.

When does registration close?
The Friday of the day of the event. There's also nothing stopping you from entering the CoRE Lounge, since you are in CoRE.

When will I know if I got in?
At the same time you choose to willingly participate and show up.

How old do you need to be to attend CoREhacks?
Old enough to be living on CoRE. You should also be in CoRE.

How much does this cost?
The CoREhacks Team? Probably something above zero. You? Zero.

I've never been to a hackathon before, can I still apply?
Of course you can! Our attendees will range from first-timers to veteran hackers. Saddle up to a new framework, refresh a stale project, or just hang out and enjoy the company you've always had.

I don't live near Binghamton University - can I still come?
Probably not, because then you don't live in CoRE.

How am I supposed to get there?
If you live on the floor, you’re good to go.

Are buses being provided?
Buses will be provided out of courtesy to all attendees. You may check the schedules here for the best route to the community.

Can high schoolers join?
Unfotunately, we cannot accept high schoolers due to liability issues and the fact that there are no high schoolers in CoRE. This is a CoRE internal hackathon after all.

My question isn't answered here.
Sucks to be you.

No, seriously. I have a question not listed here. Who do I ask?
Oh, in that case, talk to Gabe, Taylor, or Alan. Why didn't you say so sooner? This is a FAQ. That wasn't a question.
